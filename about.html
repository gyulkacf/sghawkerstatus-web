About SG Hawker Status

There's nothing worse than making your way to a hawker centre only to find it shut for cleaning. SG Hawker Status exists to solve that — a simple, no-fuss way to check hawker centre closure dates before you head out.

Why do hawker centres close?

Hawker centres in Singapore close periodically for scheduled cleaning, maintenance, and renovation works. These closures are planned in advance to ensure the centres remain clean, hygienic, and safe for both diners and stallholders.

How often do they close?

Most hawker centres undergo spring cleaning four times a year. Some also close for longer periods for renovation and upgrading works, which are less frequent but can last several months to a few years.

What is spring cleaning for hawker centres?

Since 2003, NEA has required all hawker centres in Singapore to close four times a year for spring cleaning. During these closures, stallholders clean their individual stalls while the centre undergoes a thorough deep clean of floors, drains, and shared areas. All stalls stop operating for the duration.

What if my usual hawker is closed?

Check the closure dates here before heading out. If your usual spot is closed, it's a good opportunity to explore a nearby hawker centre instead.
